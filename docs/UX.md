# UX and User Flows

## Experience principles

- Show one clear next action per screen.
- Use plain South African English before professional jargon.
- Explain why data is requested and how it will be used.
- Never hide payment, deadline, data sensitivity or cancellation terms.
- Preserve drafts on unstable connections.
- Design for 360px width, touch use and low-cost Android devices.
- Celebrate verified progress without gamifying poverty or financial hardship.

## Worker journey

### Onboarding

1. Welcome and product promise.
2. Role selection.
3. Account creation.
4. Consent and privacy explanation.
5. Capability conversation.
6. User reviews generated profile.
7. Home shows one recommended starter path.

### Opportunity discovery

Each task card shows:
- outcome;
- payment;
- estimated effort;
- deadline;
- remote or location requirement;
- readiness status;
- why it matches.

### Task detail

Before acceptance, the worker sees:
- exact deliverables;
- prerequisites;
- permitted data access;
- privacy warnings;
- revision policy;
- payment lifecycle;
- support and dispute options.

### Workbench

- Progress indicator.
- One step expanded at a time.
- Examples clearly marked as examples.
- AI assistance invoked intentionally, not continuously.
- Submission preview separates worker content from AI suggestions.
- Final confirmation states what will be shared with the business.

### Passport

Three visible evidence levels:
- Verified work
- Assessed practice
- Self-declared capability

Only verified work appears as verified. Public sharing is off by default.

## Business journey

### Create a task

1. Select a desired outcome, not a blank job post.
2. Complete template-specific inputs.
3. Review privacy and permission prompts.
4. See suggested scope, effort and payment.
5. Preview exactly what the worker sees.
6. Fund or activate the labelled sandbox state.
7. Publish or submit for moderation.

### Review work

- Compare output against rubric.
- Accept, request one specific revision, or dispute.
- Feedback prompts focus on behaviour and results, not personality.
- Re-engage option appears after completion.

## Empty, loading and failure states

Every core screen defines:
- first-use empty state;
- no-match state;
- slow connection state;
- AI unavailable state;
- permission denied state;
- recoverable form error;
- contact-support route.

## Accessibility

- Semantic landmarks and labels.
- Keyboard navigation.
- Visible focus.
- Sufficient contrast.
- No meaning conveyed by colour alone.
- Captions/transcripts for audio.
- Plain-language validation.
- Reduced-motion support.

## Content tone

Nala is encouraging, respectful and direct. It does not call users unskilled, promise guaranteed jobs, shame incomplete profiles, or imply that a worker failed because an opportunity was unavailable.
