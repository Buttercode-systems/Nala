# Trust, Safety and Privacy

## Safety objective

Nala must create legitimate economic opportunity without enabling exploitation, non-payment, deception, discrimination, unsafe data access or reputational harm.

## Prohibited tasks

- Unpaid trial work or speculative work presented as a task.
- Fake reviews, impersonation, spam or deceptive outreach.
- Harassment, intimidation or unlawful debt collection.
- Political persuasion, manipulation or disinformation.
- Access to bank accounts, passwords, one-time PINs or unrestricted financial systems.
- Processing identity documents or highly sensitive personal data without an approved, lawful workflow.
- Medical, legal, credit or other regulated decisions.
- Adult, violent, illegal or exploitative work.
- Work designed to evade platform rules, employer policies or the law.

## Fair work controls

- Payment shown before acceptance.
- Estimated effort and effective-pay warning.
- No penalty for declining a task.
- Clear revision and cancellation limits.
- One revision maximum in the MVP unless both sides agree through support.
- Businesses cannot silently expand scope.
- Repeated low-pay or abusive behaviour triggers review.

## Business verification

Risk-based checks may include:
- business registration or alternative proof of trade;
- representative identity and authority;
- business contact verification;
- sanctions/fraud signals where lawful and available;
- first-task manual review;
- payment authorisation or sandbox acknowledgement.

Verification is not a guarantee and must not be presented as one.

## Worker protection

- Minimal exposure of personal contact information.
- In-platform communication for the MVP where feasible.
- Reporting and blocking.
- Clear dispute path.
- No public negative rating without context and appeal.
- No requirement to purchase training, equipment or access to receive a task unless transparently disclosed and independently justified.

## Customer-data protection

- Businesses upload the minimum information needed.
- Demonstration data is synthetic.
- Customer identifiers are masked where possible.
- Raw files remain private and expire according to retention policy.
- Portfolio evidence is redacted and separately approved.
- Workers cannot download or retain data beyond the task need where technical controls allow.

## Disputes

Reasons include:
- non-payment;
- incomplete or poor work;
- changed scope;
- abusive communication;
- privacy breach;
- fraudulent evidence;
- unfair feedback.

The disputed state freezes final verification and payment progression until a human decision. Decisions and evidence are audit logged.

## AI safety

- AI cannot make final adverse eligibility decisions without a review route.
- Protected traits are excluded from ranking.
- Sensitive prompts and outputs are minimised in logs.
- High-risk flags are explainable to moderators.
- Users may correct material AI-generated profile information.

## Privacy baseline

Before launch, document:
- lawful purpose for each data category;
- retention period;
- deletion and export process;
- subprocessors and model providers;
- cross-border data treatment;
- incident response;
- contact route for privacy questions.

## Incident response

1. Contain access or suspend affected workflow.
2. Preserve audit evidence.
3. Assess affected users and data.
4. Correct the vulnerability or unsafe process.
5. Notify affected parties and authorities where required.
6. Record corrective and preventive actions.

This document is a product safety baseline, not a substitute for South African legal review before handling real workers, payments or customer data.
