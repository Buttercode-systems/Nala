# FNB App of the Year 2026 Submission Narrative

## Category

**Jobs & Economic Resilience**

## Submission thesis

Nala tackles the experience trap: young people cannot get work because they lack experience, but they cannot gain experience because they cannot get work.

At the same time, small businesses carry real administrative work that is neglected because it is too small for another permanent hire.

Nala converts that neglected work into safe, paid starter tasks. AI guides first-time workers through successful completion. The business verifies the result, and the worker leaves with earnings and portable proof of employability.

## One-line pitch

**Nala creates first work experience by turning neglected small-business tasks into safe paid work and verified proof of employability.**

## Demonstration story

A salon owner needs to confirm tomorrow's appointments. Nala converts the need into a controlled paid task. A young worker with no formal experience completes a readiness simulation, accepts the transparent scope, uses the AI workbench, submits outcomes, receives business verification and earns the first entry in a Proof-of-Work Passport. Nala then unlocks a higher-value receptionist-support opportunity.

## Why it belongs in the category

- Creates immediate income rather than only listing jobs.
- Creates net-new accessible work from existing business needs.
- Produces verified experience for people excluded by conventional hiring.
- Strengthens small-business operations.
- Builds a path from micro-work to more stable economic participation.
- Measures earnings, work completed, business value and progression.

## Innovation

The innovation is not an AI CV writer or general career chatbot. It is the closed loop between:

`real business demand → guided paid work → employer verification → portable proof → next opportunity`

Nala manufactures the first rung of the economic ladder.

## Impact metrics

Real metrics once pilots begin:
- workers completing a first paid task;
- total worker earnings;
- verified skills created;
- progression to higher-value work, training or formal roles;
- small businesses supported;
- business hours saved or revenue recovered;
- repeat engagement;
- dispute, non-payment and safety rates.

Never mix demo metrics with real outcomes. All submission statistics require a source or a clearly labelled pilot measurement.

## Technical strength

- Mobile-first progressive web app.
- Secure role-based Supabase architecture.
- Structured AI outputs and provider abstraction.
- Human review for verification, moderation, disputes and payment state.
- Privacy-safe evidence and auditable lifecycle transitions.
- Low-data design and future language expansion.

## Credibility boundaries

The submission must not claim:
- a live FNB integration unless authorised and working;
- a regulated wallet or escrow unless implemented through a lawful partner;
- guaranteed earnings or employment;
- national impact before validated deployment;
- multilingual or offline capability beyond what is tested.

## Judge takeaway

Within five minutes, a judge should understand that one Nala task creates value on both sides:

- the business gets useful work completed;
- the worker earns money;
- the worker gains verified experience;
- the platform generates a credible next step.

## Competition build standard

The product should be usable without a developer manually changing records. The full demo journey must work from onboarding to task creation, acceptance, completion, verification, earnings record and progression.
