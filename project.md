# Nala — Master Project Blueprint

## 1. Product definition

Nala is a verified work-experience and first-income platform for young people who are excluded from formal employment by the experience barrier.

It converts routine small-business work into controlled starter tasks, assists workers through successful completion, verifies the outcome with the business, records earnings, and converts the result into portable proof of employability.

## 2. North-star promise

**Earn your first income. Build your first proof of work. Unlock your next opportunity.**

## 3. Core thesis

The primary barrier is not always a lack of potential or training. It is the absence of trusted evidence that a person can perform real work reliably.

Nala creates that evidence instead of merely helping users describe themselves better.

## 4. Users

### Worker

An unemployed or underemployed person who may have informal capability but little or no verified experience.

Primary needs:
- immediate, legitimate income opportunities;
- clear guidance;
- safe entry-level tasks;
- evidence of reliability and skill;
- a visible path to better work.

### Business

A small or emerging business with repetitive work that is neglected, delayed, or performed inconsistently.

Primary needs:
- simple delegation;
- predictable cost;
- controlled access to information;
- quality assurance;
- trustworthy workers;
- measurable operational value.

### Opportunity partner

An employer, training provider, NGO, youth programme, or enterprise sponsor that needs verified evidence of participant capability and progression.

## 5. Core loop

1. A business selects a defined outcome.
2. Nala converts the need into a safe task package.
3. Eligible workers are matched based on capability, readiness, availability and location where relevant.
4. The worker completes a simulation or readiness check.
5. The worker accepts the task with payment, scope and deadline clearly disclosed.
6. The AI workbench guides the worker without concealing who performed the work.
7. The worker submits required evidence.
8. Automated checks flag quality, privacy and policy concerns.
9. The business reviews and verifies the result.
10. Payment status and earnings are recorded.
11. The worker's Proof-of-Work Passport is updated.
12. Nala recommends the next task, learning action or formal opportunity.

## 6. MVP boundaries

The MVP proves one complete, credible economic loop. It is not a broad job board, social network, bank, lender, open freelance marketplace, or general learning platform.

Included:
- worker and business onboarding;
- conversational capability profile;
- fixed task templates;
- task creation and acceptance;
- AI-guided workbench;
- evidence submission;
- business review and verification;
- simulated or partner-backed payment state;
- earnings ledger;
- Proof-of-Work Passport;
- progression recommendations;
- admin moderation and dispute workflow.

Excluded until validated:
- lending or credit decisions;
- stored-value wallet;
- claimed FNB integration;
- unrestricted user-created task categories;
- live mentor marketplace;
- full USSD workflow;
- broad multilingual claims without evaluation;
- autonomous job applications;
- access to business bank accounts or unrestricted customer systems.

## 7. Initial task catalogue

1. Appointment confirmations
2. Quote follow-up preparation
3. Invoice-reminder preparation
4. Spreadsheet cleanup
5. Social-media content preparation

Every template specifies:
- intended business outcome;
- permitted and prohibited inputs;
- estimated duration;
- fair payment guidance;
- worker prerequisites;
- exact deliverables;
- evidence requirements;
- quality rubric;
- privacy treatment;
- escalation conditions.

## 8. Differentiation

Nala is not differentiated by AI-generated CVs. It is differentiated by creating trusted work evidence through real economic activity.

The defensible system is:
- structured demand generation from neglected business work;
- guided entry-level execution;
- auditable human contribution;
- business verification;
- portable proof of work;
- performance-based progression.

## 9. Trust principles

- No unpaid trial work.
- Payment and scope disclosed before acceptance.
- Businesses and workers are identity- and risk-checked proportionately.
- Sensitive customer information is minimised and redacted.
- AI recommendations remain explainable and contestable.
- No ranking based on protected characteristics.
- Disputes and low-confidence decisions route to human review.
- Portfolio evidence must respect business and customer confidentiality.

## 10. Success metrics

### North-star metric

Number of workers who complete a verified paid task and progress to a higher-value opportunity within 90 days.

### Worker metrics

- onboarding-to-first-task conversion;
- time to first earnings;
- task completion rate;
- verified skills gained;
- repeat work rate;
- progression rate;
- income generated;
- dispute and exploitation rate.

### Business metrics

- time to task fulfilment;
- accepted-output rate;
- repeat task rate;
- hours saved;
- value recovered or created;
- business satisfaction;
- dispute rate.

### Platform health

- supply-demand balance;
- payment success;
- fraud rate;
- moderation response time;
- AI override rate;
- retention by cohort;
- cost per verified outcome.

## 11. Business model

Workers are not the primary paying customer.

Potential revenue:
- service fee paid by businesses;
- managed workforce programmes funded by enterprises, NGOs or public-interest partners;
- verified-talent access for employers;
- outcome dashboards for programme sponsors;
- workflow subscriptions for repeat business users.

## 12. Delivery principle

Build vertical slices, not disconnected screens. The first release is successful only when a business task can move from creation through completion, verification and worker progression using real persisted data.

## 13. Competition narrative

Nala belongs in **Jobs & Economic Resilience** because it does not merely advertise existing opportunities. It creates accessible first work, produces immediate income, helps small businesses, and converts economic participation into evidence that unlocks longer-term employment.

## 14. Definition of ready to code

Coding begins when:
- MVP scope is frozen;
- user flows and acceptance criteria are documented;
- database ownership and row-level security are designed;
- AI actions and human-review gates are specified;
- safety policies and prohibited tasks are agreed;
- the first demonstration journey is fixed;
- the delivery backlog is prioritised.
